# Release Gates

## Gate philosophy

Milestones are capability gates, not calendar promises. A gate passes only when the product proves the next thesis step without depending on hand-wavy future work.

## Gates

| Version | Gate name | Must have |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `0.1.0` | MVP vertical slice | Frozen chapter contracts, integrated map/combat/pacing/memory/telemetry, one region, one boss, flashback and reclaim loops |
| `0.3.0` | Combat depth | A richer loop than a flat question app, phase or tempo variation, a revenge-like retry path |
| `0.5.0` | Memory palace proof | Recursive submaps or nested spatial identity, place-linked concepts, memorable flashback and reclaim behavior |
| `0.8.0` | Predictive and institutional layer | Reliable fail-risk signals, readiness diagnostics, school or cohort reporting primitives |
| `1.0.0` | Full product | Broad curriculum coverage, multiple regions and bosses, stable narrative and progression vocabulary, polished growth surfaces |

## Hard rules

- Do not advance a gate with a feature pile that does not prove the thesis.
- Do not turn 0.1.0 into a broad content dump.
- Do not let later milestones erase the memory-palace identity.

## Why these gates matter

- `0.1.0` proves the idea is real.
- `0.3.0` proves the game loop is not shallow.
- `0.5.0` proves spatial memory is actually helping.
- `0.8.0` proves the data can become a business wedge.
- `1.0.0` proves the product is coherent end to end.
